Rules

Core Rules

Core patterns

Core sentence

subject + verb + object

mi mo mek. I eat food.

ta re buka? Do you read a book?

To be

Nari uses one word for am, is, are, was, and were forms.

be means to be, am, is, or are.

mi be al dom. I am at home.

Tense

Present uses the verb alone. Past adds -a. Future adds -u.

mi ko. I go.

mi koa. I went.

mi kou. I will go.

Negation and questions

Put na before the verb. Questions keep normal order and add a question mark.

mi na ni. I do not know.

wer lis be? Where is she?

Word creation

Take one root and add one ending. The root stays the same.

tava means teach. taver means teacher.

-er person, -in tool, -nes quality, -ing action.

Pronouns

Pronouns are short and never change form.

mi I, ta you, mu we, lu they.

li can mean a person when gender or identity does not matter.

Possession

Fast form: use possessive pronouns before the noun.

mai buka my book. tai dom your house.

Clear form: use ov. buka ov mi book of me.

Use su only for have: ta su tel.

Usage patterns

Continuous action

Put du before a verb for an action happening right now.

lis du re. She is reading.

mu du vora. We are working.

Modals

Modal words come before the main verb.

mi kan ko. I can go.

ta musu suma. You must study.

Clauses

dat links ideas and also works like who, which, or that.

mi ni dat lih be guda. I know that he is good.

mava dat ra the man who speaks.

Comparison

Use mor, les, mos, and lest.

dis kar mor fasa. This car is faster.

dat dom mos bi. That house is biggest.

Compounds

Join short words when a new idea is easier than a new root.

vida-kala video call.

sol-gala sunglasses.

Style

Prefer the shortest sentence that still carries the meaning.

mi na ni. I do not know.

mi kou la tomd. I will come tomorrow.
